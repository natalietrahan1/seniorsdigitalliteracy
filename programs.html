Our Programs

Computer Basics

What you'll learn: How to use your computer easily for everyday tasks.

  • Turning your computer on and off, using the mouse and keyboard.
  • Navigating the internet safely and finding information.
  • Setting up email and simple ways to fix common issues.

Smartphone Basics

What you'll learn: How to use your phone for calls, messages, and more.

  • Making calls, sending texts, and managing your contacts.
  • Connecting to Wi-Fi, taking photos, and using helpful apps.
  • Adjusting your phone's settings to make it work best for you.

Online Safety

What you'll learn: How to stay safe and confident when using the internet.

  • Recognizing and avoiding common online scams and tricks.
  • Keeping your passwords strong and your personal information private.
  • Shopping and banking online securely.

Curriculum

Objective: Participants will feel comfortable turning on their device, navigating its basic interface, and connecting to the internet.

  • Introduction: What is a computer/smartphone? Basic components. Turning devices on/off.
  • Navigating the Interface: Touchscreen gestures (tap, swipe, pinch), mouse & keyboard basics, understanding icons and menus.
  • Connectivity: Connecting to Wi-Fi networks, understanding mobile data.
  • Basic Settings: Adjusting screen brightness, volume, font size.
  • Power & Care: Charging devices, battery life tips.

Objective: Participants will be able to send and receive emails and messages, and manage their contacts.

  • Email Basics: Setting up an email account, composing, sending, receiving, replying, forwarding.
  • Texting (SMS/MMS): Sending and receiving text messages on a smartphone.
  • Messaging Apps: Introduction to apps (e.g., WhatsApp), sending messages, photos, voice notes.
  • Managing Contacts: Adding, editing, and finding contacts.

Objective: Participants will be able to navigate the internet to find information and understand the importance of online safety.

  • What is the Internet?: How to open and use a web browser.
  • Searching for Information: Using search engines (e.g., Google), effective search terms.
  • Understanding Websites: Identifying reliable sources, recognizing different types of websites.
  • Introduction to Online Safety: Why safety is crucial, understanding basic risks.

Objective: Participants will be equipped to identify common online threats and take steps to protect their personal information.

  • Recognizing Common Scams: Phishing emails, suspicious phone calls, fake links.
  • Strong Passwords: Creating and managing secure passwords.
  • Online Privacy: Understanding what information you share, basic privacy settings.
  • Secure Online Transactions: Safe practices for online banking and shopping.

Objective: Participants will be able to use their devices for practical tasks like managing photos, getting directions, and finding information.

  • Photos & Videos: Taking, viewing, organizing, and deleting media.
  • Maps & Navigation: Using map applications to find locations and get directions.
  • Accessing Information: Reading news, checking weather, finding local services online.
  • Introduction to Apps: What are apps? How to download and uninstall them safely from app stores.

Objective: Participants will be able to connect with others via video calls, feel more confident troubleshooting basic issues, and know where to find further resources.

  • Video Calls: Using platforms like WhatsApp, FaceTime, or Facebook Messenger to connect with family and friends.
  • Review & Consolidation: Recap of key skills learned throughout the course.
  • Troubleshooting Common Issues: Simple fixes for slow devices, frozen apps, connectivity problems.
  • Useful Online Resources: Exploring helpful websites for hobbies, health, etc.
  • Course Wrap-up: Open Q&A, certificate presentation, and encouragement for continued learning.

What you need to bring

To make the most of your session, please bring:

  • Your device: Your smartphone, tablet, or laptop that you wish to learn on.
  • Its charger: Ensure your device is ready to go and stays powered up.
  • A notebook and pen: Handy for jotting down helpful tips and instructions.
  • Any questions you have: Don't hesitate to bring your specific curiosities or challenges!
